Ghassan Kanafani

He is a Palestinian novelist, storyteller and journalist, and he is considered one of the most famous Arab writers and journalists in the twentieth century,and from his quotes:..."Everything in this world can be robbed and stolen, except one thing; this one thing is the love that emanates from a human being towards a solid commitment to a conviction or cause." ,"My political position springs from my being a novelist In so far as I am concerned, politics and the novel are an indivisible case and I can categorically state that I became politically committed because I am a novelist not the opposite."

About his life:

1936 - 1948

He is the only one among his siblings. He was born in Acre, and he narrates from his birth that his mother, when labor came, was unable to reach her bed before giving birth to her newborn, and Al-Walid nearly suffocated and this happened on April 9, 1936. He joined the Frere School in Jaffa and was envied because he was studying French. . His family lived in the Mansheya neighborhood of Jaffa, which is adjacent to Tel Aviv, and he witnessed the first incidents of friction between Arabs and Jews. Therefore, the father carried what his wife and children needed and brought them to Acre, and he returned to Jaffa. The family stayed there from October 1947 until it was one of the nights of late April 1948 when the first attack took place on the city of Acre.

1948 - 1955

The family moved to Lebanon, and they arrived in Sidon and stayed for forty days, after which they moved to Aleppo, then to Damascus, and there began another cruel phase in the family’s life, and after the improvement of financial conditions and his father opened an office to practice law Besides his studies, he took a job as a rehearsal in some newspapers and sometimes editing, and participated in the Palestine program on the Syrian Radio and wrote some poetry and plays.When he finished high school, he worked as a teacher in refugee schools and joined the University of Damascus to study Arabic literature and was entrusted at that time with organizing the Palestine Pavilion at the Damascus International Fair, and most of what was exhibited in it was Ghassan's personal effort.

1955 - 1972

. He went to Kuwait in 1953, then moved to Beirut to work for Al-Hurriya Magazine (1961), in charge of the cultural section in it, then he became the editor-in-chief of the Lebanese newspaper (Al-Muharar), and published in it (The Palestine Supplement). When the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine was established in 1967, he founded A magazine bearing the name "Al-Hadaf Magazine"
